Natural resources in the future.

Changing the perception of the Natural resources in the future

Natural resources refer to those resources that occur in their original and natural form within the environment, without any human intervention. They take years to form and are vital for our survival. Mother Earth is abundant with natural resources that develop on this planet using its surrounding environment. While some resources like water and air are used for our survival, others like coal, gas, and oil satisfy our daily needs.
However, with the world's population fast approaching 8 billion, resource management has become a major concern. While conservation is essential, it is equally important to understand that as the population grows, the demand for resources will also increase. The industrialization of third-world countries further exacerbates this problem.
As societies grow and become more modernized, their consumption of natural resources also increases. Planning for an increase in demand is crucial, and we must strive to find sustainable solutions.
There are two types of natural resources based on their stage of development:
Potential Resources: These resources are not yet in use but have the potential to be utilized in the future. For instance, petroleum in sedimentary rocks remains a potential resource until drilled out and put to use.
Actual Resources: These are resources that have been surveyed, quantified, qualified, and are currently in use. Examples include wood processing, which depends on technology and cost.
In conclusion, it is essential to recognize the importance of natural resources and their management. As we strive to meet the needs of a growing population, it is crucial to find sustainable ways to utilize and conserve these resources for future generations.
