Maritime in the future.

Changing the perception of the Maritime in the future

The maritime industry is often criticized for its role in environmental pollution and emissions, which is not surprising considering that it carries over 90% of world trade. The massive ships, most of which are powered by fossil fuels, are anything but environmentally friendly. However, the industry is nearing a turning point in its mindset, with major companies embarking on "green" projects, ordering new ships with dual-fuel technologies or even zero emissions. Let's take a look at the latest initiatives and success stories.
Van Dam Shipping, in cooperation with India's Tata Steel, is planning to launch a hydrogen-powered vessel by 2024, which would be completely CO2 free and save around 3000 tons of CO2 per year compared to fossil fuel-powered ships.
With these projects and achievements, the maritime industry is undoubtedly heading in the right direction towards a greener future. To discuss these initiatives, new ideas, challenges, and share good practices, the maritime networking expert IGGS Group is organizing an exceptional gathering in Rotterdam – the 2nd Envirotech for Shipping Forum on October 5th and 6th.
