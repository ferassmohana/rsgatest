Trade in the future.

Changing the perception of the Trade in the future

The current pattern of economic integration and fracturing across different economies and sectors is best described as "reglobalization." While globalization is far from finished, it is changing in nature through countries' emphasis on stronger regional links and the formation of economic blocs for sensitive and strategically important sectors. The full-scale decoupling from China is neither achievable nor desirable for the G7 and like-minded partners. Trade policy has an important role to play in underpinning the positive aspects of a reglobalized world and in balancing geopolitical competition and cooperation. This can be achieved not just through coordinated efforts to strengthen supply-chain resilience but also by enabling countries worldwide to benefit from the twin transitions to green and digital economies. This briefing paper draws on insights from expert roundtable discussions and a high-level speaker series under the umbrella of the Chatham House Global Trade Policy Forum.
