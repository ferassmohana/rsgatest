What’s Is RSGA ?

Who we are

Red Sea and the Gulf of Aden (RGSA) contains eight founding member states: Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Jordan, Yemen, Sudan, Somalia, Eritrea, and Djibouti.
The headquarter of the General Secretariat is located in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. The council convenes through a rotating presidency and is composed of three key agencies: Supreme Council, Ministerial Council, and General Secretariat.
All member nations that border the Red=Sea or the Gulf of Aden are part of the regional organization known as the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden, which was established in January 2020 to improve the relationships between its members by focusing on and tackling projects that will boost cooperation and unity.

Governance

RDGAC operates through a governance model with five layers The highest authority of the council comprised of the kings and heads of states of the member states of the council Composed of the foreign ministers of the member states Filled on a rotating basis for a four-year term from each member state according to the alphabetical order of the states' names, first Secretary General shall be from the host country Permanent or temporary entities focused on executing specific tasks and/or initiatives
