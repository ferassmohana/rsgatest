About The State

Eritrea is a country located on the Horn of Africa with a coastal location on the Red Sea. Its name reflects the importance of its history and culture, as Mare Erythraeum is Latin for "Red Sea." Eritrea's ports provide access to the gold, coffee, and slaves sold by traders in the Ethiopian highlands to the south. In the second half of the 20th century, Eritreans had to free themselves from Ethiopian rule to create their own state. After a nearly three-decade-long war of independence, Eritrea became a sovereign country in 1993. Although Eritreans forged a common national consciousness during the struggle for independence, they now face the task of overcoming ethnic and religious differences to improve their country's poverty made worse by years of drought, neglect, and war. The capital and largest city of Eritrea is Asmara (Asmera).
