About The State

Sudan is a country located at the crossroads of Sub-Saharan Africa and the Middle East, stretching across the Red Sea. It shares borders with seven countries, including Libya and Egypt to the north, Chad to the west, the Central African Republic to the southwest, South Sudan to the south, Ethiopia to the southeast, and Eritrea to the east. Khartoum, the capital city of Sudan, is where the White and Blue Niles meet and merge to become the Nile River that flows all the way to the Mediterranean Sea via Egypt. Sudan has a Sahelian belt with the desert in the far north, fertile land in the Nile valleys, and the Gezira, as well as farming and livestock herding areas across the rest of the country from Darfur to Kassala via Blue Nile and Kordofan States. Although Sudan is sparsely populated, it has crucial water sources like the River Nile that traverses the country from south to north. The country shares international borders with Egypt, Eritrea, Ethiopia, South Sudan, Central African Republic, Chad, and Libya.
