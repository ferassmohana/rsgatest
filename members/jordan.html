About The State

Jordan is a young nation situated on an ancient land that has been home to many civilizations throughout history. Its borders are separated from ancient Palestine by the Jordan River, and the region has played a prominent role in biblical history. Jordan is home to many ancient biblical kingdoms such as Moab, Gilead, and Edom, as well as the famous red stone city of Petra, which was the capital of the Nabatean kingdom and the Roman province of Arabia Petraea. British traveler Gertrude Bell described Petra as “like a fairy tale city, all pink and wonderful.”

Jordan was part of the Ottoman Empire until 1918 and later became a mandate of the United Kingdom until it gained independence in 1946. The country is among the most politically liberal in the Arab world, and its rulers have expressed a commitment to maintaining peace and stability despite the challenges facing the region.
