About The State

Djibouti is a small but strategically important country located on the northeast coast of the Horn of Africa. It is situated on the Bab el Mandeb Strait, which separates the Red Sea from the Gulf of Aden. It was formerly known as French Somaliland and the French Territory of the Afars and Issas before gaining independence from France in 1977 and adopting the name Djibouti. The capital, Djibouti city, is built on coral reefs that extend into the southern entrance of the gulf. Other major towns include Obock, Tadjoura, Ali Sabieh, Arta, and Dikhil. Despite its small size, Djibouti is geopolitically significant as it hosts a modern deepwater port that serves Indian Ocean and Red Sea traffic and a French naval base. Additionally, Djibouti city is the railhead for the only line serving Addis Ababa, the capital of neighboring Ethiopia.
