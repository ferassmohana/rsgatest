April 17 ,2023 Governance: Supreme Council

The Supreme Council is the highest authority of the council and is composed of the kings and heads of states of the member states of the council. The chairmanship of the Supreme Council rotates for each term pursuant to the alphabetical order of the names of member states, with an exception for the host country, which chairs the council for the first term. The Supreme Council convenes once a year, and extraordinary meetings may be held if necessary. Meetings are considered valid if attended by two-thirds of the council members.
Competencies of the Supreme Council include approving the organizational structure of the council, approving the internal by-laws and the financial, administrative, and other regulations of the council, approving the draft budget of the council and its other agencies and the final accounts, appointing the Secretary General of the council and his deputy based on objective and specific criteria approved by the council, provided that the Secretary General and his deputy are of different nationalities, reviewing the reports and recommendations submitted by the Ministerial Council and taking necessary measures, approving the rules and mechanisms for cooperation with regional and international sides, and considering the possibility of cooperation with states or organizations to achieve the council's goals and objectives, provided the Supreme Council unanimously approves the same.
Each member state has only one vote in the Supreme Council, and resolutions are issued by consensus.
