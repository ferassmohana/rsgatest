April 17 ,2023 Governance: The Ministerial Council

The Ministerial Council is composed of the foreign ministers of the member states. It convenes biannually or once a year at maximum in the rotating presidency state. If that is not possible, it will convene in the host country, unless agreed otherwise. It may hold extraordinary meetings where necessary, and both the ordinary and extraordinary meetings are valid if attended by two thirds of its members.
The competencies of the Ministerial Council include proposing strategies and policies related to the Council's goals and objectives to submit to the Supreme Council and supervising their implementation. It also submits recommendations to the Supreme Council regarding referred subjects and its point of view concerning any other issue related to the Council's objectives. It supervises the implementation of the Council's programs and projects and studies issues related to the Council's activities and areas of work to achieve its goals without prejudice to the sovereignty and rights of Member States.
The Ministerial Council reviews the periodic reports submitted on the progress of work of the General Secretariat and supervises the preparation of the Council's organizational structure, internal by-laws, financial, administrative, and other regulations, as well as the preparation of the draft budget for submission to the Supreme Council for approval. It approves the appointment of two assistants to the Secretary General and any other competency delegated by the Supreme Council and tasks assigned within the framework of the Council's objectives.
Each member state has only one vote in the Ministerial Council, and the resolutions of the Ministerial Council are issued by consensus.
