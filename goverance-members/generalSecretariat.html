April 17 ,2023 Governance: The General Secretariat

The Secretary General of the Council shall be appointed on a rotating basis from each member state according to alphabetical order, unless otherwise agreed upon. However, the first Secretary General shall be from the host country. The Secretary General shall serve for four years, and their term shall be renewable only once. The deputy Secretary General shall serve for three years, with their term being renewable for one period only.
The Secretary General shall supervise the progress of work at the General Secretariat, which includes two assistants who belong to a nationality other than that of the Secretary General and their deputy. The duties, competencies, professional tasks, and term of service of the assistants shall be determined by the regulations.
The General Secretariat shall comprise employees appointed on the basis of equitable geographical distribution to reflect the participation of all member states in the tasks assigned to the Secretariat. The number of employees shall be sufficient to undertake the affairs and meetings of the Council and its secretary.
Competencies of the Secretary General include preparing the draft organizational structure, internal by-laws, and financial, administrative, and other regulations of the Council for submission to the Supreme Council. They are responsible for preparing studies related to the Council's work, supervising the preparation of progress reports on the work of the Supreme Council and the Ministerial Council, preparing the draft budget and final accounts of the Council, depositing and registering a copy of the Charter or any amendment with the United Nations Organization, and representing the Council before regional and international organizations and agencies based on tasks assigned by the Supreme Council or the Ministerial Council. The Secretary General is also responsible for any other tasks assigned to them by the Supreme Council or Ministerial Council.
Tasks of the General Secretariat include undertaking preparations for the meetings of the Supreme Council and the Ministerial Council, monitoring the implementation of the resolutions of both councils, preparing studies on cooperation and coordination among the Member States of the Council in fields related to Council objectives, preparing periodic progress reports on the work of the Council, circulating resolutions and documents issued by the Council and its affiliated agencies to the Member States of the Council, and any other tasks assigned by the Supreme Council or Ministerial Council.
